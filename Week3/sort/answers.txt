sort1 uses: Bubble Sort

How do you know?: Because Bubble sort takes the least amount of time when the numbers are already sorted and a lot of time when the elements are in random order.

sort2 uses: Merge Sort

How do you know?: Because Merge Sort is slower compared to other sort algorithms for the smallest tasks and goes through the whole sorting process even if the array is sorted.

sort3 uses: Selection Sort

How do you know?: Because Selection Sort never takes more than O(n) swaps to do the process.
